### 1. The PMP Eco-System

The current PMP exam is balanced across **predictive (waterfall)**, **agile**, and **hybrid** methodologies. You must understand how to apply project management principles in all these contexts.

- **Agile/Hybrid is Key**
  - Approximately **half of your exam** will be focused on agile and hybrid approaches.
  - Master the concepts in the **Agile Practice Guide** (Scrum, Kanban, XP, Lean).

- **Process Groups & Knowledge Areas (PMBOK Guide ‚Äì 6th Edition)**
  - Even though the exam is organized by the three new domains, you still need to understand:
    - The **49 processes**
    - The **Inputs, Tools & Techniques, and Outputs (ITTOs)**
    - How they all relate across the five process groups and ten knowledge areas.

- **The PMP Mindset**
  - The PMP exam is a **scenario-based** test.
  - Answer questions based on **PMI best practices**, not just your personal work experience.
  - Always think:
    - *‚ÄúWhat is the PM‚Äôs responsibility?‚Äù*
    - *‚ÄúWhat is the next best thing to do?‚Äù*

### 2. Deep Dive Topics

  - Understanding the **Communication Channels** formula


## üìù Phase 2: Practice and Review (Core Focus)

Bridge the gap between **knowing the content** and **applying it under exam pressure**.

### 1. Practice Exams

The most critical study activity is taking **full-length, timed practice exams**.

- **Simulate the Real Exam**
  - The PMP exam is **180 questions over 230 minutes**.
  - Aim for **3‚Äì5 full-length mock exams** under realistic testing conditions.
  - This builds **stamina** and helps you **manage time** effectively.

- **Start with a Diagnostic Test**
  - Take an early practice test to identify your weakest domains:
    - People
    - Process
    - Business Environment
  - Use this to **tailor your study plan**.

- **Review, Review, Review**
  - The value is **not** in the score ‚Äî it is in the **review process**.
  - For every question (right or wrong):
    - Read the explanation.
    - Understand **why** the correct answer is right.
    - Understand **why** the other options are wrong.
  - This reinforces the **PMI mindset**.

### 2. Formula Sheet (Brain Dump)

On test day, you will want to quickly jot down key formulas (EVM, communication channels, etc.).

- **Practice Writing Daily**
  - Create a ‚Äú**brain dump**‚Äù sheet every day for a few minutes.
  - This reinforces memory and boosts confidence on exam day.

- **Key EVM Formulas**

  ```
  CV  (Cost Variance)              = EV - AC
  SV  (Schedule Variance)          = EV - PV
  CPI (Cost Performance Index)     = EV / AC
  SPI (Schedule Performance Index) = EV / PV
  ```

## Communication Channels Formula

```text
Number of Channels = n (n - 1) / 2
where n = number of stakeholders

  
  EAC (Estimate At Completion)     = several variants depending on scenario
  VAC (Variance At Completion)     = BAC - EAC
```
