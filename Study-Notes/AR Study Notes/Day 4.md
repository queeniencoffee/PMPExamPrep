# Day 4 - Section 7:  Introduction to Traditional Predictive Project Management

## Video 71 - Introduction to the Process Groups

The video outlines the 49 processes defined in the "Process Groups: A Practice Guide" by PMI and emphasizes the difference between predictive and adaptive project management.    Defined Scope: It explains that predictive projects maintain a defined scope throughout execution, making them suitable for large, fixed projects, such as skyscrapers.
### Five Process Groups:
        Initiating: Key processes include developing the project charter and identifying stakeholders.
        Planning: This group consists of 24 processes that form a comprehensive project management plan for managing scope, budget, and resources.
        Executing: Involves 10 processes focused on carrying out project work.
        Monitoring and Controlling: Comprises 11 processes that ensure the project aligns with the initial budget, schedule, and scope.
        Closing: Encompasses the formal completion of the project.

Complexity Reassurance: The instructor reassures students that although the processes may seem complex, they will be explained in detail throughout the course, highlighting their importance for effective project management.

Other key highlights:  Traditional v.s. Agile v.s. incremental v.s. iterative
