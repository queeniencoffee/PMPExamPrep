## 2.3 The 12 Project Management Principles

These are **behavioral guidelines**, not processes. You are expected to **apply** them in scenario questions.

1. **Stewardship**  
   Be a responsible steward of financial, human, environmental, and reputational resources.  
   - Ethics, integrity, compliance, sustainability.

2. **Team**  
   Build a collaborative, motivated, and respectful team environment.  
   - Psychological safety, diversity, inclusion, clear roles.

3. **Stakeholders**  
   Engage stakeholders proactively; understand needs, expectations, influence.  
   - Manage **relationships**, not just documents.

4. **Value**  
   Focus on delivering **value and benefits**, not only outputs.  
   - Regularly ask: â€œIs this still valuable?â€ and recommend changes.

5. **Systems Thinking**  
   View the project as part of a **larger system**.  
   - Interdependencies, feedback loops, unintended consequences.

6. **Leadership**  
   Use **situational, service-oriented, and empowering** leadership.  
   - Adapt style; lead by example.

7. **Tailoring**  
   Adapt **methods, tools, and life cycle** to the project context.  
   - No â€œone size fits all.â€

8. **Quality**  
   Build **quality into** processes and deliverables.  
   - Prevention > inspection; continuous improvement.

9. **Complexity**  
   Recognize and navigate technical, organizational, and social complexity.  
   - Use experimentation, simplification, feedback.

10. **Risk**  
    Address both **threats and opportunities**.  
    - Risk management is continuous and proactive.

11. **Adaptability & Resilience**  
    Be prepared to **adjust plans** and **bounce back** from setbacks.  
    - Flexibility under change and uncertainty.

12. **Change**  
    Accept that projects both **drive and experience change**.  
    - Manage impacts on people, processes, and the organization.

## 2.4 The 8 Performance Domains

Performance domains are **ongoing focus areas** you must manage well for project success.  
They are **interdependent and not strictly sequential**.

---

### 2.4.1 Stakeholder Performance Domain

**Focus:** Identify, analyze, and **engage stakeholders**.

**Key elements:**

- Identify stakeholders; analyze **power, interest, influence**.  
- Understand needs, expectations, and potential impact.  
- Build trust, manage expectations, handle conflict.  
- Choose appropriate **communication channels and frequency**.

**Exam cues:**  
â€œIncreasing support,â€ â€œreducing resistance,â€ â€œmisalignment of expectationsâ€ â†’ **Stakeholder engagement**.

---

### 2.4.2 Team Performance Domain

**Focus:** **People, collaboration, and performance.**

**Key elements:**

- Build a **cohesive, high-performing team**.  
- Clarify roles, responsibilities, and working agreements.  
- Encourage **self-organization, empowerment, accountability**.  
- Address conflict constructively; promote **psychological safety**.

**Exam cues:**  
Questions about **motivation, conflict resolution, team norms, leadership style**.

---

### 2.4.3 Development Approach & Life Cycle Performance Domain

**Focus:** How you **develop and deliver** the product and **manage change**.

**Key elements:**

- Choose development approach:
  - **Predictive**, **Iterative**, **Incremental**, **Agile**, **Hybrid**.  
- Choose **project life cycle** structure and phases.  
- Align approach to:
  - Requirements stability  
  - Risk level and uncertainty  
  - Need for feedback and speed  
  - Organizational environment

**Exam cues:**  
â€œHigh uncertainty / changing requirementsâ€ â†’ **Agile / Hybrid**.  
â€œStable, well-defined requirementsâ€ â†’ **Predictive / Hybrid**.

### 2.4.4 Planning Performance Domain

**Focus:** How you **plan**â€”as an **ongoing, evolving** activity.

**Key elements:**

- Planning is **iterative and collaborative (rolling wave)**.  
- Integrate scope, schedule, cost, quality, resources, risk, communications, procurement, stakeholders.  
- Plans exist at **different levels**:
  - Roadmap  
  - Release plan  
  - Iteration / sprint plans  
  - Detailed task plans  
- Keep plans **realistic, flexible, and value-aligned**.

### 2.4.5 Project Work Performance Domain

**Focus:** **Executing and managing the work.**

**Key elements:**

- Coordinate people, resources, and processes to deliver outputs.  
- Manage **issues, changes, impediments**.  
- Ensure compliance with policies, standards, governance.

**Exam cues:**

### 2.4.6 Delivery Performance Domain

**Focus:** Deliver **outputs** that create **outcomes and value**.

**Key elements:**

- Manage requirements, scope, acceptance criteria.  
- Validate deliverables with stakeholders.  
- Confirm outputs lead to **intended outcomes**.  
- Handle **operational readiness, handover, transition**.

**Exam cues:**  
Questions about **acceptance, validation, handover, benefits realization**.

---

### 2.4.7 Measurement Performance Domain

**Focus:** **Measure performance** to support decision-making.

**Key elements:**

- Choose meaningful **metrics and indicators**:
  - Schedule, cost, quality, throughput, business outcomes.  
- Use **baselines and thresholds**.  
- Use **dashboards, reports, information radiators**.  
- Avoid gaming metrics; focus on **insight and decisions**.

**Exam cues:**  
Use of **KPIs, dashboards, performance reports, EVM, trends**.

### 2.4.8 Uncertainty Performance Domain

**Focus:** **Risk, ambiguity, and complexity.**

**Key elements:**

- Manage **known risks** (threats & opportunities).  
- Handle **ambiguity** and emergent risks.

## 2.5 Tailoring in PMBOK 7

**Tailoring** = customizing how you apply practices to a specific project.

### Tailoring steps (exam-friendly)

1. **Understand the context**  
   - Organization, culture, governance, regulations.

2. **Assess the project**  
   - Size, complexity, risk, uncertainty, stakeholders.

3. **Select development approach & life cycle**  
   - Predictive, agile, hybrid; phase structure and cadence.

4. **Tailor methods, tools, and artifacts**  
   - Meetings, reports, documentation level, controls, metrics.

5. **Review and adapt continuously**  
   - Adjust tailoring when **conditions change**.

**Exam cues:**  
â€œWhat should the PM do first?â€ â†’ **Assess context and needs before picking tools.**  
â€œOne-size-fits-allâ€ â†’ usually wrong; **tailoring** is expected.
