## ğŸ“Œ Risk Response Strategies (PMP / PMI-ACP)

In PMI terminology, these are **risk response strategies**.  
They differ for **threats (negative risks)** and **opportunities (positive risks)**.

---

## 1. Strategies for Threats (Negative Risks)

| Strategy  | Type   | Description                                                                 | Simple Example |
|----------|--------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| Avoid    | Threat | Change the plan so the risk **cannot occur**.                              | Remove risky scope or choose a different technology. |
| Mitigate | Threat | **Reduce the probability and/or impact** of the risk.                      | Add prototypes, extra testing, or training to lower the chance of failure. |
| Transfer | Threat | Shift the **impact/ownership** of the risk to a **third party** (usually by paying a fee). | Buy insurance, use warranties, or sign a fixed-price contract with a vendor. |
| Accept   | Threat | **Do nothing proactive** beyond monitoring; may set aside reserves.        | Acknowledge the risk and create a contingency budget if it occurs. |
| Escalate| Threat | Risk is **outside the projectâ€™s scope or authority**; send to higher level (program/portfolio). | Raise a regulatory or organizational risk to senior management. |

**Notes:**
- **Accept** can be:
  - **Passive accept** â€“ acknowledge and monitor only.
  - **Active accept** â€“ establish **contingency reserves** (time/cost).

---

## 2. Strategies for Opportunities (Positive Risks)

| Strategy | Type        | Description                                                              | Simple Example |
|---------|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------|
| Exploit | Opportunity | Change the plan so the opportunity **definitely occurs**.                | Assign your best team so an early-finish opportunity is guaranteed. |
| Enhance | Opportunity | **Increase probability and/or impact** of the opportunity.               | Add resources or tools to make a performance gain more likely. |
| Share   | Opportunity | **Partner** with a third party to jointly realize the opportunity.       | Form a joint venture to enter a new market together. |
| Accept  | Opportunity | Take advantage **if it happens**, but **do not invest** to pursue it.    | Use extra capacity if a vendor delivers early, but donâ€™t spend to accelerate them. |
| Escalate| Opportunity | Opportunity is better managed at **program/portfolio/organizational** level. | A strategic market opportunity passed to corporate leadership. |

## 4. Exam Tips

- On PMP questions, always ask:
  1. Is this a **threat** or an **opportunity**?
  2. Is the PM **allowed to act**, or should the risk be **escalated**?
- If the goal is to **completely eliminate** a threat â†’ think **Avoid**.  
- If the goal is to **reduce** probability/impact â†’ think **Mitigate**.  
- If the goal is to **move impact to a third party** â†’ think **Transfer**.  
- If the risk is **outside project authority** â†’ think **Escalate** (threat or opportunity).

Check Sheet (Tally Sheet)

Collects frequency data on defects or issues (how many, how often, where).

Histogram

Histogram sorted from largest to smallest with cumulative % line.

Plots results over time with control limits and spec limits.

Used to see if a process is in control or showing special-cause variation.

Trend over time; used to spot trends, shifts, or cycles in quality data.

Scatter Diagram

Plots two variables to see if there is a correlation (e.g., temp vs defects).

Helps quantify relationships (positive, negative, none).
