# Agile Team Roles (Scrum-Oriented, PMP Context)

This document summarizes the **key members in an Agile (Scrum-style) team**, what they do, and includes a compact table suitable for PMP study notes or a brain-dump sheet.

---

## 1. Product Owner (PO)

**Main responsibility:**  
Owns the **product value** and **what gets built**.

**What they do:**

- Define and **own the Product Backlog**  
  - Write and refine user stories  
  - Define acceptance criteria  
- **Prioritize** work based on:
  - Business value  
  - Risk  
  - Dependencies  
- Act as the **voice of the customer / business**:
  - Gather feedback from users, stakeholders, and the market  
- Make **scope and priority decisions**:
  - Decide what goes into each iteration / sprint  
- Clarify requirements for the team during development  
- **Accept or reject** completed work based on the Definition of Done and acceptance criteria  

> **Exam tip:** If a question involves **prioritizing features, maximizing value, or deciding what to build**, the correct role is usually the **Product Owner**.

---

## 2. Scrum Master / Agile Team Coach

**Main responsibility:**  
Owns the **process** and **team effectiveness**.

**What they do:**

- Facilitate Agile ceremonies:
  - Daily stand-up  
  - Sprint planning  
  - Sprint review  
  - Sprint retrospective  
- **Remove impediments** that block the team  
- **Coach the team** on Agile principles and practices  
- Protect the team from:
  - Scope creep  
  - Unnecessary interruptions  
- Help improve **team collaboration** and **continuous improvement**  
- Support the Product Owner with:
  - Backlog refinement  
  - Stakeholder communication  

> **Exam tip:** If a question involves **removing impediments, improving the process, or coaching the team in Agile**, think **Scrum Master / Agile Coach**.

---

## 3. Developers / Cross-Functional Delivery Team

**Main responsibility:**  
Own **how** the work is done and **delivering the product increment**.

**Who they are:**  
A cross-functional group that may include:

- Software developers / engineers  
- Testers / QA engineers  
- UX / UI designers  
- Business analysts  
- DevOps / infrastructure engineers  
- Data engineers, etc.

**What they do:**

- Estimate and **commit to work** each iteration  
- Design, build, test, integrate, and document the product  
- **Self-organize**:
  - Decide who does what  
  - Decide how the work is performed  
- Maintain quality:
  - Unit tests  
  - Automation  
  - Peer reviews  
  - Adherence to the Definition of Done  
- Collaborate closely with the Product Owner to clarify requirements  
- Continuously improve practices, tools, and ways of working  

> **Exam tip:** In the PMBOK / PMP context, they are often simply called **“Developers”** or the **“Agile team.”**

---

## 4. Key Supporting Roles (Not Core Scrum Roles)

These roles are important but typically **not** listed as core Scrum team roles:

### Stakeholders

- Executives, business sponsors, end users, operations, legal, compliance, etc.  
- Provide:
  - Requirements  
  - Feedback  
  - Funding  
  - Constraints (regulatory, legal, technical)

### Agile Project Manager / Project Manager in Agile Context (PMP View)

- Often present in **larger programs or organizations** using Agile at scale  
- Coordinates:
  - Across multiple teams  
  - Risks, budget, and high-level schedule  
- Works with:
  - Product Owner(s)  
  - Scrum Masters / Agile Coaches  
- Focuses on:
  - Governance  
  - Reporting  
  - Alignment with organizational strategy
